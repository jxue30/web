What Happens During Sleep

Researchers do not completely understand what happens during sleep yet, but so far, they have broken it up into four stages. The first stage is N1, and it usually only lasts one to five minutes. It's what we associate with dozing off, and someone can be easily awaken from this stage. The next stage is N2, and heart rate and breathing slows down. This stage usually lasts 10-25 minutes. The next stage, which is also the most important one, is N3. Heart rate and breathing slows down further, and the body relaxes and heals. This stage is linked to creativity, memory, and problem solving abilities. N3 usually lasts 20-40 minutes. The last stage is REM, which stands for Rapid Eye Movement. During this stage, the entire body is paralyzed except for the muscles that control eye movement and breathing. This stage can be from 10 to 60 minutes long, and brain activity speeds up. As the night progresses, each stage in the sleep cycle tends to lengthen. An average person goes through four to six cycles per night. Source and further reading

Effects of Sleep Deprivation

Sleep deprivation can have deprecating effects on the body. It can result in changes in energy levels, mood, focus, and memory. Sleep deprivation also weakens the immune system, and chronic sleep deprivation increases the risk for stroke, cardiovascular diseases, and mental illnesses such as depression and anxiety. It can also cause weight gain since being sleep-deprived increases apatite and decreases metabolism. Sleep deprivation also speeds up aging and shortens life expectancy. Sleep deprivation can be caused by insomnia or other sleep disorders, schedules, and using electronics before bedtime. Sleeping Habits

There are many ways to fall asleep quicker and increase your quality of sleep. One is to reduce blue light exposure in the evenings. Some devices have settings to block blue light, and reducing screen time is another way to reduce exposure. Establishing a bedtime routine is also helpful, along with waking up and sleeping at the same times each day. Calming down before sleeping and doing something relaxing such as reading a book also might help you fall asleep faster, and some people use breathing exercises.
